## Website Color Theme Plan

To ensure a cohesive and visually appealing website that reflects the Three Circles brand, the following color palette and theme guidelines are recommended for both light and dark modes. These are based on the provided brand colors and best practices for accessibility and modern design.

### 1. Brand Color Palette
**Primary Red:** #f90000  
**Primary Black:** #231f20  
**Primary White:** #ffffff

**Supporting Colors:**
- **Light Gray:** #f5f5f5 (for backgrounds, cards, and subtle separation in light mode)
- **Dark Gray:** #18181a (for backgrounds and surfaces in dark mode)
- **Accent Gray:** #b0b0b0 (for borders, dividers, and muted text)

### 2. Light Theme
- **Background:** #ffffff (Primary White)
- **Primary Text:** #231f20 (Primary Black)
- **Headings/Highlights:** #f90000 (Primary Red)
- **Secondary Text:** #4a4a4a (Dark Gray for readability)
- **Cards/Sections:** #f5f5f5 (Light Gray)
- **Borders/Dividers:** #b0b0b0 (Accent Gray)
- **Buttons:**
	- **Primary:** #f90000 (Red background, white text)
	- **Secondary:** #231f20 (Black border/text, white background)

### 3. Dark Theme
- **Background:** #18181a (Dark Gray)
- **Primary Text:** #ffffff (Primary White)
- **Headings/Highlights:** #f90000 (Primary Red)
- **Secondary Text:** #b0b0b0 (Accent Gray)
- **Cards/Sections:** #231f20 (Primary Black)
- **Borders/Dividers:** #4a4a4a (Dark Gray)
- **Buttons:**
	- **Primary:** #f90000 (Red background, white text)
	- **Secondary:** #ffffff (White border/text, dark background)

### 4. Usage Guidelines
- Use **Primary Red** for call-to-action buttons, highlights, and important links.
- Use **Primary Black** and **Primary White** for text and backgrounds, depending on the theme.
- Maintain high contrast for accessibility (especially text on backgrounds).
- Use supporting grays for subtle separation, cards, and less prominent UI elements.
- Ensure all interactive elements (buttons, links) have sufficient color contrast and clear hover/focus states.

### 5. Accessibility
- All color combinations should meet WCAG AA contrast standards.
- Test both themes for readability and visual comfort.
